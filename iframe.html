Background

Scott Stedman and Erin Lankau were blogging partners. They co-authored at least 6 articles together, published on their respective Medium accounts, from late 2017 to February 2018.

On February 9, 2018, Scott Stedman published an article based on research done by Erin Lankau without her concent and despite her [evident] private and public objections!

From the numerous Twitter conversations and web archive materials gathered below, it is clear that Lankau refused the publication of her research, with the claims made by Stedman, because it "push[ed] a claim that doesn't hold up"! She explicitly called Stedman out for publishing unverified information which, in turn, will lead to her as a researcher and will expose her to risks she was not willing to take.

In fact, Erin Lankau felt so affraid by Stedman's betrail that she deliberately errased all traces of her publications and social media presence from the internet. She never resurfaced on the internet in her own name ever since. Evidence suggest long-lasting mental breakdown following Stedman's betrail.

This reconstruction of events is based on the public comments made at the time by followers of both Lankau and Stedman, who both privately and publically deplored Stedman to ammend the wrongdoing done to his once partner and co-author.

The chain of events detailed below and the onslaught of accusations made against Stedman took place one month before Stedman began "courting" for the attention of Natasha Bertrand, who would later become the "mentor" who catapulted Stedman from obscurity into a Twitter mega-influencer. Some of Stedman's critics believe that it was in-fact the Lankau affair and the wave of public accusations that followed which motivated Stedman to seek validation as the "protégé" of a star journalist.

February 23, 2018

Scott Stedman publishes his Amazon book "Real News" in which he dedicates a whole chapter to Simona Mangiante. In bewildering literary gymnastics peppered with much bravado, Stedman elaborates on his previously published social media "research", on Mangiante's family sources and the many journalists, lawyers, law enforcement agents and government officials who were happy with his work. Curiously enough, only in his chapter about Mangiante he finds it necessary to stress that he "stands behind every word that was written". He promisses that the full story of Mrs. Papadopoulos will be revealed soon.

Link to this spot

April 6, 2018

Scott Stedman holds a Q&A session on Reddit (AMA) titled I'm Scott Stedman, UC Irvine senior and independent journalist who has broken stories in the Russia investigation. AMA! In the Q&A, Stedman is asked about what happened with Erin Lankau. His answer downplays Lankau's accusations stating "she changed her mind about wanting to write the piece about Deripaska". He also adds that she "threatened a lawsuit against [him] among other things".

When another user insists, stating how they "find it odd, verging on suspiceous that [Lankau] then deleted all her social media, including her Medium account and dissapeared off the face of the planet", Stedman replied "I believe some health issues might be at play", aluding to Lankau's mental breakdown following his betrail.

In a reflection on Stedman's actions against Lankau, a prominent blogger privy to the affair noted: "it takes a certain kind of person to accuse someone of mental illness after stealing their work".

October 4, 2018

Scott Stedman publishes his first article about Simona Mangiante on Medium, accusing her for misleading the public about her employment background and her age, referencing his own research and unnamed sources among Mangiante's family.

Link to this spot

October 10, 2018

Matthew Mosk and Kaitlyn Folmer of ABC News publish a readdress article, debunking all but one of the accusations made by Stedman. The two confirm that Mangiante did not lie about her employment history. Moreover, given the motives of the family sources Stedman used, their credibility is put in serious doubt. Mangiante confessed to have scraped 3 years her age.

Link to this spot

October 26, 2018

Motivated by the shadow cast on his journalistic abilities by Mosk and Folmer's debunking ABC News piece, Stedman publishes his second article on Mangiante. The new piece brings forth speculations on work visa violations circulating on social media, inconsistencies he found in Mangiante's Linkedin account and repetition of Mangiante's dishonesty about her age. The "scoop" is information about a report filed to ICE by no other than the same unnamed family sources, who are now said to be linked to her husband George Papadopoulos. The article culminates in the solidification of the allegations that Mangiante is suspected by authorities to be a Russian spy.
